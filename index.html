Purple-Striped Jellyfish

The purple-striped jelly, Chrysaora colorata, is a jellyfish species in California with purple stripes on its 2-foot wide bell.

Habitat

These jellies live off the coast of California, from Bodega Bay to San Diego, in open waters.

A visual representation of the range of their habitat.

Diet

Purple-striped jellies eat mainly zooplankton, fish eggs, larval fish, and sometimes other smaller jellies. When prey touches their tentacles, they sting the prey to paralyze it, before eating it. The sting of the purple-striped jelly is extremely painful to humans, but not fatal.

Fun Facts

  • Some fish (like ocean sunfish) and turtles are immune to the sting of the jelly
  • They have high concentrations of carbon and nitrogen in their arms
  • Young cancer crabs are known to attach themselves to jellies and feed on parasites living on the jellies

Visit them at the Monterey Bay Aquarium (where their life cycle was first fully discovered!) or read about them on Wikipedia.
